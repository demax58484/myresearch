Research Interests

I study quantum many-body physics and quantum information, with a focus on driven cold-atom systems, time crystals, and quantum error correction. My work combines realistic modeling of cold-atom experiments with mathematical tools for understanding robustness and phases of matter in out-of-equilibrium systems.

Current Projects

Interacting Anderson transitions in kicked Bose–Einstein condensates
I investigate how interactions and trapping potentials reshape dynamical Anderson metal–insulator transitions in kicked quantum gases, connecting realistic simulations to current cold-atom experiments.
Cavity-induced time crystals with time-independent Hamiltonians
Slides (PDF) (Under construction)
I study spin chains in optical cavities that realize time-crystal behavior using an effectively time-independent Hamiltonian, mapping Floquet physics into an extended-zone cavity picture with experimentally accessible signals.
Robust quantum codes from Knill–Laflamme signature vectors
I develop optimization and geometric tools on Stiefel manifolds to search quantum code spaces using Knill–Laflamme coefficients, with the goal of identifying robust, symmetry-structured codes.
Atomtronic transistor dynamics
I model coherent matter-wave emission in triple-well “transistor” geometries, clarifying the role of resonances and interactions in controlling output coherence for future atomtronic devices.

Selected Publications

Many-Body Anderson Metal-Insulator Transition using Kicked Quantum Gases
Jun Hui See Toh, Mengxin Du, Xinxin Tang, Ying Su, Tristan Rojo, Carson O. Patterson, Nicolas R. Williams, Chuanwei Zhang, and Subhadeep Gupta · arXiv:2305.14817 · 2023
Demonstrates a synthetic many-body Anderson metal–insulator transition in kicked cold-atom gases and maps out the phase diagram directly connected to ongoing experiments. [arXiv]
Coherent Matter Wave Emission from an Atomtronic Transistor
Sasanka Dowarah, Mengxin Du, Alan Zanders, Shengwang Du, Michael Kolodrubetz, and Chuanwei Zhang · arXiv:2510.03398 · 2025 (preprint)
Studies coherent transport in a triple-well Bose–Einstein condensate, showing transistor-like behavior and identifying how interaction strength controls matter-wave coherence. [arXiv]
Characterizing Quantum Codes via the Coefficients in Knill–Laflamme Conditions
Mengxin Du, Chao Zhang, Yiu-Tung Poon, and Bei Zeng · arXiv:2410.07983 · 2024 (accepted to npj Quantum Information on Nov 24, 2025)
Introduces a signature-vector framework for Knill–Laflamme coefficients, enabling systematic exploration of quantum code spaces and revealing new codes and symmetry-preserving paths between known codes. [arXiv]
